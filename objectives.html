Strategic Objectives

Five core objectives guide our work to advance the economics of transformative AI

Objective Details

1. Research Excellence

We conduct rigorous economic research on the most pressing questions related to transformative AI. Our work spans theoretical modeling, empirical analysis, and policy evaluation, addressing critical questions about:

  • How AI will affect labor markets, wages, and employment
  • The impact on productivity growth and economic development
  • Distributional consequences and inequality dynamics
  • Optimal policy responses to AI-driven economic change

2. Education and Field Building

We are building a vibrant community of scholars focused on the economics of transformative AI. Through courses, workshops, seminars, and collaborative research, we:

  • Train graduate students and postdoctoral researchers in AI economics
  • Convene scholars from economics, computer science, and policy
  • Foster collaboration between UVA and global research institutions
  • Develop educational materials and curriculum on AI economics

3. Policy Translation

We bridge the gap between academic research and policy implementation by:

  • Engaging with policymakers at local, national, and international levels
  • Producing policy briefs and accessible research summaries
  • Providing expert testimony and consultation on AI economic policy
  • Collaborating with government agencies and international organizations

4. Public Understanding

We believe informed public discourse is essential for navigating AI's economic transition. We enhance public understanding through:

  • Public lectures, seminars, and educational events
  • Accessible writing in popular media and online platforms
  • Engagement with business leaders and industry stakeholders
  • Development of educational resources for diverse audiences

5. Innovation in Methods

We pioneer new research methodologies that leverage AI itself to advance economic research:

  • Developing AI agents as co-scientists for economic research
  • Automating literature reviews and research synthesis
  • Creating new computational tools for economic analysis
  • Exploring how AI can accelerate the research process itself
