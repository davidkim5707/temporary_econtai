Core Research Areas

Advancing economic understanding across six interconnected domains

Research Area Details

Labor Market Transformation

As AI systems become increasingly capable, they will fundamentally reshape labor markets. Our research examines how automation affects different occupations and skill levels, analyzes the pace and pattern of labor displacement, and develops policy frameworks to support workers through the transition.

Key questions include: Which jobs are most vulnerable to AI automation? How can we design education and training systems for an AI economy? What social safety nets are needed to support displaced workers?

Macroeconomic Implications

Transformative AI could drive unprecedented productivity growth while also creating new challenges for economic stability and distribution. We analyze AI's impact on GDP growth, productivity dynamics, and income inequality, examining both optimistic and pessimistic scenarios.

Research questions include: How will AI affect long-run economic growth? What are the distributional consequences of AI-driven productivity gains? How should monetary and fiscal policy adapt to an AI economy?

AI Safety & Economic Governance

Effective governance of transformative AI requires understanding the economic trade-offs involved in safety measures and regulatory frameworks. We develop economic frameworks for evaluating AI safety investments and design governance mechanisms that balance innovation with risk management.

Key research areas: Economic valuation of AI safety measures, optimal taxation of AI development, international coordination on AI governance, and mechanisms for ensuring equitable access to AI benefits.

Economic Research Automation

AI tools can accelerate economic research itself. We develop and deploy AI agents that assist in literature reviews, data analysis, model development, and research dissemination. This "AI for economic research" agenda both advances our understanding of AI's capabilities and accelerates our ability to study AI's economic impacts.

Projects include: Developing autonomous research assistants, automating systematic literature reviews, creating AI tools for econometric analysis, and exploring AI-human collaboration in research.

Financial Sector Implications

AI is rapidly transforming financial services, from algorithmic trading to credit allocation. We study how AI affects financial market dynamics, assess systemic risks from AI-driven financial systems, and develop regulatory frameworks to ensure financial stability.

Research focus: AI in algorithmic trading, credit risk assessment with AI, systemic vulnerabilities in AI-driven financial markets, and regulatory responses to AI in finance.

Economics of AI Agents

As AI agents become economic actors—making decisions, engaging in transactions, and participating in markets—we need new economic theory to understand their behavior and interactions. We develop theoretical frameworks for AI agent economics and study the implications for market design, regulation, and economic welfare.

Key questions: How do AI agents interact in markets? What regulatory frameworks are needed for AI economic agents? How should property rights and liability be assigned to AI systems?
